# 4 · AI Acceptable-Use Policy

*The one page every employee reads. Plain language, no legalese.*

## You can

- Use **approved AI tools** to help you work — drafting, summarizing, brainstorming, coding, analysis.
- Treat AI output as a **starting point you're responsible for**, not a final answer. You own what you ship.
- Ask the [AI Enablement Hub](08-operating-model.md) for help, tools, or training anytime.

## You must

- **Match the tool to the data.** Only put data into a tool approved for its [classification](02-data-classification.md). When unsure, treat it as Confidential and ask.
- **Keep a human in the loop** for anything that affects a person, a customer, money, or a legal/compliance outcome.
- **Check it.** Verify facts, figures, code, and citations before relying on them. AI is confidently wrong sometimes.
- **Disclose AI use** where it matters — customer-facing content, research outputs, and decisions of record.

## You must not

- Paste **customer, personal, payment, or regulated data** into public/consumer AI tools. Ever.
- Use AI to make a **final decision about a person** (hiring, credit, discipline) without human judgment and a route to appeal.
- Use unapproved "shadow" tools for anything above Public data. If a tool you want isn't approved, [request it](06-intake-and-approval.md) — that's a five-minute path, not a dead end.
- Present AI-generated work as independently verified when it isn't.

## If something goes wrong

Report it (a bad output that caused harm, a data exposure, a tool acting strangely) via the [incident process](07-incident-response.md). **Reporting in good faith is never penalized** — quiet problems are the expensive ones.
